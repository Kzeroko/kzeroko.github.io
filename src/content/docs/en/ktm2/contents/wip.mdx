# Future Plans for KTM2

KTM2 is still deep in development. While the roadmap has shifted, I have some significant updates and strategic changes to share with you all.

**The Version Shift: Why I am moving to 1.20.1** For the past two to three years, I remained a "legacy resident" of version 1.19.2. At the time, I was frankly exhausted by the constant stream of redundant Minecraft updates and the friction they introduced to mod development. I believed staying put would allow me to focus on content rather than chasing the "version treadmill."

However, with the upcoming implementation of Vulkan rendering and the broader evolution of the modding ecosystem, I’ve decided to move **KTM2 forward to version 1.20.1**. Although 99% of the porting is technically complete, this transition requires a massive overhaul of my assets. Much of the UI, textures, and mod localizations are being reworked from the ground up to meet the standards I envision for this project.

**Current Development Focus:** KTM2 aims to be more than just a modpack; it's a handcrafted world. I am currently pouring my energy into:

1.  **Original World-Building:** You won’t find generic prefab mods here. Every town, generated structure, and dungeon is **100% original and exclusive to KTM2**, ensuring a unique visual and exploratory experience.
2.  **Narrative Immersion:** I am refining the NPC systems, branching dialogues, and quest lines. My goal is to create a living, breathing world where every interaction feels meaningful.
3.  **Immersive Crafting Mechanics:** I am moving away from traditional crafting grids. Planned features include **immersive metallurgy and alchemy systems**, turning resource processing into an engaging, tactile experience.

Thank you for your continued patience. Embracing 1.20.1 as the new foundation will allow me to push KTM2 further than ever before.
